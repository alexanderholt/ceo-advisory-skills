# Regulation Z — Special Rules for Private Education Loans (Subpart F)

**Citation:** 12 C.F.R. Part 1026, Subpart F (§§1026.46-1026.48)
**Source:** Electronic Code of Federal Regulations (ecfr.gov), pasted directly by user
**Current as of:** 3/13/2026

---

## §1026.46 Special disclosure requirements for private education loans

### (a) Coverage
Requirements apply to private education loans as defined in §1026.46(b)(5). A creditor may optionally comply for extensions of credit for post-graduation expenses (bar exam, residency, relocation).

### (b) Definitions

**Covered educational institution:** An institution of higher education (regardless of accreditation status), including its agents, officers, employees, and institution-affiliated organizations as defined by 20 U.S.C. 1019.

**Private education loan:** An extension of credit that:
- Is not made, insured, or guaranteed under title IV of the HEA
- Is extended to a consumer expressly for postsecondary educational expenses
- Does not include open-end credit or any loan secured by real property or a dwelling
- Does not include certain short-term extensions by the educational institution itself (90 days or less, or one year or less with no interest)

**Preferred lender arrangement:** Same meaning as in 20 U.S.C. 1019.

### (c) Form of disclosures
- Must be clear and conspicuous
- Transaction disclosures (§§1026.47(b) and (c)) must be in writing, grouped together, segregated from everything else
- "Finance charge" and interest rate must be more conspicuous than other disclosures (except creditor identity)
- Electronic disclosures permitted subject to E-Sign Act

### (d) Timing of disclosures
1. **Application/solicitation disclosures** (§1026.47(a)): On or with any application or solicitation. For telephone applications, may be disclosed orally or mailed within 3 business days.
2. **Approval disclosures** (§1026.47(b)): Before consummation, on or with any notice of approval. If mailed, must be mailed with notice. If by telephone, mailed within 3 business days. If in person, provided at that time.
3. **Final disclosures** (§1026.47(c)): After consumer accepts the loan per §1026.48(c)(1).
4. **Receipt of mailed disclosures:** Consumer considered to have received them 3 business days after mailing.

### (e) Basis of disclosures and use of estimates
- Disclosures must reflect the terms of the legal obligation
- If information is unknown, creditor must use best information reasonably available and clearly state the disclosure is an estimate

## §1026.47 Content of disclosures

### (a) Application or solicitation disclosures
Must include:

1. **Interest rates:**
   - Rate or range of rates actually offered at time of application
   - Whether fixed or variable
   - If variable: limitations on adjustments (or lack thereof), statement that actual rate could be higher or lower
   - Whether rates are typically higher without cosigner

2. **Fees and default costs:**
   - Itemized fees required to obtain the loan
   - Fees, rate changes, and principal adjustments for defaults or late payments

3. **Repayment terms:**
   - Loan term (period of regularly scheduled payments)
   - Payment deferral options (or statement that deferral is not available)
   - For each deferral option while enrolled: whether interest accrues, whether interest may be deferred and added to principal

4. **Cost estimates:** Total cost example calculated as total of payments:
   - Using highest disclosed rate with all applicable finance charges
   - Using $10,000 amount financed ($5,000 if creditor only offers loans under $10,000)
   - Calculated for each payment option

5. **Eligibility:** Age or enrollment requirements

6. **Alternatives to private loans:**
   - Statement that consumer may qualify for Federal aid under Title IV
   - Federal interest rates and whether fixed or variable
   - Where to get more information (including ED website)
   - Statement about school-specific loan benefits

7. **Consumer rights:** If approved, terms available for 30 days (except rate adjustments and permitted changes)

8. **Self-certification:** Must complete self-certification form from institution before consummation

### (b) Approval disclosures
On or with notice of approval, must disclose §1026.18 information plus:

1. **Interest rate:** Applicable rate, fixed/variable, limitations on adjustments

2. **Fees and default costs:** Same as application disclosures

3. **Repayment terms:**
   - Principal amount approved
   - Loan term
   - Payment deferral option chosen and other available options
   - Payments required while enrolled (based on deferral chosen)
   - Unpaid interest that will accrue while enrolled
   - Bankruptcy statement
   - **Total amount of payments** estimated using: (A) applicable rate, (B) maximum rate or 25% if no maximum, (C) if no maximum, statement that there is no maximum and estimate will be higher if rate increases
   - **Maximum monthly payment** based on maximum rate or 25%

4. **Alternatives to private loans** (same as application disclosures)

5. **Consumer rights:**
   - 30-day acceptance period with specific expiration date and method of acceptance
   - Rates and terms may not change during this period (except permitted changes)

### (c) Final disclosures
After consumer accepts, must disclose:

1. Interest rate (per approval disclosures)
2. Fees and default costs (per approval disclosures)
3. Repayment terms (per approval disclosures)
4. **Cancellation right:**
   - Consumer can cancel without penalty before cancellation period expires
   - Loan proceeds will not be disbursed until cancellation period expires
   - Specific cancellation date and method(s) for cancelling
   - If cancellation by mail permitted, mailed request deemed timely if placed in mail by cancellation date
   - **This disclosure must be more conspicuous than all other disclosures except finance charge, interest rate, and creditor identity**

## §1026.48 Limitations on private education loans

### (a) Co-branding prohibited
A creditor (other than the institution itself) shall not use the institution's name, emblem, mascot, or logo in marketing private education loans in a way that implies the institution endorses the creditor's loans.

**Safe harbor:** Marketing does not imply endorsement if it includes a clear and conspicuous disclosure, equally prominent and closely proximate to the institution reference, that the institution does not endorse the creditor's loans and the creditor is not affiliated with the institution.

### (b) Endorsed lender arrangements
If a creditor and institution have an endorsement arrangement (not prohibited by other law), the co-branding prohibition does not apply if marketing clearly discloses that loans are not offered by the institution but are made by the creditor.

### (c) Consumer's right to accept

1. **30-day acceptance period:** Consumer has 30 calendar days after receiving approval disclosures to accept

2. **Rate and terms locked:** Except for permitted changes, rates and terms may not change during this period or until disbursement, whichever is earlier

3. **Exceptions NOT requiring re-disclosure:**
   - Withdrawal for fraud or legal prohibition
   - Index-based rate changes
   - Changes that unequivocally benefit the consumer
   - Loan amount reduction based on changed cost of attendance or financial aid (with corresponding term adjustments)

4. **Exceptions REQUIRING re-disclosure:**
   - If creditor accommodates a specific consumer request for different terms
   - Must provide new approval disclosures and new 30-day acceptance period
   - Cannot withdraw original offer unless consumer accepts the modified terms

### (d) Consumer's right to cancel
Consumer may cancel without penalty until midnight of the third business day following receipt of final disclosures. **No funds may be disbursed until the three-business-day period has expired.**

### (e) Self-certification form
For private education loans for postsecondary expenses while student attends an institution, creditor must obtain the ED self-certification form (developed under HEA section 155), signed by the consumer in written or electronic form, before consummation.

### (f) Provision of information by preferred lenders
Creditors with preferred lender arrangements must annually provide covered institutions with disclosure information (§1026.47(a)(1)-(5)) for each type of private education loan planned for the July 1 – June 30 period. Must provide by the later of April 1 or within 30 days of entering/learning of the arrangement.

## Relevance to Desata

Key questions for ClarifiED:
- **Does displaying lender rates/terms constitute a "solicitation"?** If so, application disclosures (§1026.47(a)) may be triggered
- **Co-branding restrictions:** If institutions use ClarifiED, lender marketing through the platform must not imply institutional endorsement
- **Self-certification requirement:** The ED self-certification form must be completed before any private education loan can be consummated, regardless of how the borrower found the lender
- **30-day acceptance and 3-day cancellation:** These consumer protections apply to every private education loan

# State Financial Aid Offer Letter Law Survey

**Purpose:** Identify states that mandate standardized financial aid offer letter formats.
**Last reviewed:** March 2026

---

## Federal Context

### Current Federal Landscape

There is **no binding federal requirement** for a standardized financial aid offer letter format as of March 2026, though there have been significant regulatory and legislative efforts:

1. **ED "College Financing Plan" (voluntary):** The Department of Education has published a model financial aid offer form (formerly the "Shopping Sheet," now the "College Financing Plan"). Institutions may use it voluntarily; it is not mandated.

2. **Financial Value Transparency (FVT) rule (2024):** ED finalized regulations requiring institutions to disclose program-level outcomes data, but these are disclosure requirements for outcomes, not standardized offer letter formats.

3. **Proposed federal legislation:**
   - **"Understanding the True Cost of College Act"** (Sens. Grassley, Smith, Ernst) — would mandate standard language and format for financial aid offers
   - **"College Cost Reduction Act"** (Rep. Foxx, 2024) — includes transparency provisions
   - **December 2025 bill** — would require standardized content, terminology, and formatting that distinguishes between grants, scholarships, loans, work-study, required costs, and other costs
   - None of these have been enacted as of March 2026

4. **College Cost Transparency Initiative (CCT):** NASFAA and 10+ higher education associations launched a voluntary initiative establishing principles and standards for aid offers. Not legally binding.

### GAO Findings
A GAO report found that institutions are **not** providing clear and standard information in financial aid offers, with wide variation in terminology, format, and what information is included.

## States with Enacted Offer Letter / Award Notification Laws

### States with Mandatory Standards

Based on available information, the state landscape for mandated offer letter formats is **thin**. Unlike PLL laws (where a wave of state legislation followed the 2007 scandals), standardized offer letter mandates have been pursued primarily at the federal level. The following states have enacted provisions related to offer letter transparency:

| State | Citation / Description | Year | Key Provisions |
|-------|----------------------|------|----------------|
| **Indiana** | IC 21-12-17 (College Financial Aid Notification Act) | 2019 | Requires state-funded institutions to provide financial aid offers using a standardized template developed by the Commission for Higher Education. Must separately itemize gift aid, loans, and work-study. Must include net cost calculation. |
| **Louisiana** | La. R.S. 17:3129.8 | 2018-2019 | Requires Board of Regents to develop a uniform financial aid award letter format for public institutions. Must clearly distinguish grants from loans. Part of broader student transparency legislation. |
| **Texas** | Tex. Educ. Code §61.0777 | 2019 | Requires the Texas Higher Education Coordinating Board to establish a standard financial aid award letter format for public institutions. Must clearly distinguish gift aid from self-help aid. Must include total cost of attendance and net cost. |
| **California** | Cal. Educ. Code §69999 et seq. | 2022 | Requires the Student Aid Commission to develop a standardized financial aid award letter template. Applies to public and private nonprofit institutions participating in state aid programs. Must include specified cost and aid categories. |

### States with FAFSA Completion Requirements (Related but Distinct)

These states require FAFSA completion for graduation but do not mandate offer letter format:

| State | Requirement | Notes |
|-------|-------------|-------|
| Louisiana | FAFSA completion or opt-out for graduation | First state to enact (2018) |
| Texas | FAFSA completion or opt-out for graduation | Mandatory |
| Alabama | FAFSA completion or opt-out for graduation | Mandatory |
| Illinois | FAFSA completion or opt-out for graduation | Mandatory |
| Maryland | FAFSA encouragement (district responsibility) | Softer requirement, no graduation mandate |
| California | FAFSA encouragement (district responsibility) | Softer requirement |
| Colorado | FAFSA encouragement (district responsibility) | Softer requirement |
| Indiana | FAFSA encouragement (district responsibility) | Softer requirement |

Note: FAFSA completion requirements are distinct from offer letter standardization. They are included here for context because they represent the broader state-level financial aid transparency movement.

## ClarifiED Relevance

1. **Indirect relevance:** ClarifiED is a loan recommendation engine, not an offer letter tool. However, if ClarifiED's recommendations are integrated into or presented alongside financial aid offers, state offer letter laws may affect presentation format.
2. **Standardized terminology:** States that require standardized terminology (distinguishing grants from loans, using "net cost" calculations) establish expectations that ClarifiED's outputs should be consistent with. Using different terminology than what students see in their offer letters would create confusion.
3. **Potential opportunity:** The lack of federal standardization and the GAO findings about inconsistent offer letters represent a market context where ClarifiED's clear, standardized approach to loan information adds value.

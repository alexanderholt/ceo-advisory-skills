# Dodd-Frank Act §1031 — UDAAP (Unfair, Deceptive, or Abusive Acts or Practices)

**Citation:** 12 U.S.C. §5531
**Source:** Dodd-Frank Wall Street Reform and Consumer Protection Act
**Fetched from:** uscode.house.gov

---

## Authority (§5531(a))

The CFPB may take action to prevent covered persons from committing unfair, deceptive, or abusive acts or practices in connection with consumer financial transactions.

## Rulemaking (§5531(b))

The Bureau may issue rules identifying unlawful practices and establish preventative requirements.

## Unfairness Standard (§5531(c))

The Bureau cannot declare conduct unfair unless it:
- Causes substantial consumer injury
- The injury is not reasonably avoidable by consumers
- The injury is not outweighed by countervailing benefits to consumers or competition

## Abusive Standard (§5531(d))

An act or practice is **abusive** when it either:

1. **Materially interferes** with the ability of a consumer to understand a term or condition of a financial product, OR

2. **Takes unreasonable advantage** of:
   - A consumer's lack of understanding of the material risks, costs, or conditions of the product
   - A consumer's inability to protect their own interests in selecting or using a financial product
   - A consumer's reasonable reliance on a covered person to act in the consumer's interests

## Consultation (§5531(e))

The CFPB must consult with federal banking agencies on consistency with regulatory objectives.

## Relevance to Desata

The "abusive" prong (§5531(d)(2)) is particularly relevant:
- If students rely on ClarifiED recommendations without understanding the methodology's limitations, this could constitute taking "unreasonable advantage" of their "lack of understanding"
- If students reasonably believe ClarifiED acts in their interest (rather than being a neutral tool), the "reasonable reliance" prong applies
- Clear disclosures about methodology, limitations, and the nature of recommendations are essential to mitigate UDAAP risk

# HEA Section 485(a) / 20 U.S.C. Section 1092(a) — Institutional Information Disclosures

**Citation:** Higher Education Act of 1965, Section 485(a), codified at 20 U.S.C. Section 1092(a)
**Source:** https://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/20/1092
**Date:** Originally enacted 1965. Substantially amended by HEOA (2008) and subsequent reauthorizations. Current through 2026.

---

## Overview

Section 485(a) of the Higher Education Act (HEA) requires every institution participating in Title IV federal student aid programs to provide certain information to prospective and enrolled students. This is one of the broadest disclosure mandates in federal higher education law and establishes the baseline for what students must be told about costs, financial assistance, and institutional characteristics.

The information must be made readily available through appropriate publications, mailings, and electronic media.

## Who Must Comply

Every **eligible institution** participating in any program under Title IV of the HEA. This includes:
- Public and private four-year colleges and universities
- Community colleges
- For-profit institutions
- Trade and vocational schools

...that participate in federal financial aid programs (Pell Grants, Direct Loans, Federal Work-Study, etc.).

## Who Must Receive the Information

- **Enrolled students** (including those attending less than full time)
- **Prospective students** (upon request)

Institutions must designate an employee or group of employees available on a full-time basis to assist students and prospective students in obtaining this information.

## Required Disclosures — Financial Assistance

Under Section 1092(a)(1), institutions must make available information regarding:

### Financial Aid Programs
- A description of all **federal, state, local, private, and institutional** student financial assistance programs available to students at the institution
- For each program: **terms and conditions**, including eligibility requirements and criteria for selecting recipients
- A description of the **rights and responsibilities** of students receiving financial assistance

### Cost of Attendance
- **Tuition and fees** (distinguishing between in-state and out-of-state if applicable)
- Estimated costs of **books, supplies, and equipment** (including the typical cost of materials for the institution's most popular programs)
- Estimated costs of **room and board** (or housing and food for students not living on campus)
- Estimated costs of **transportation**
- Any **additional costs** for the program in which the student is enrolled or expresses an interest

### Net Price
- Information on the institution's **net price calculator** (required under the Higher Education Opportunity Act of 2008)
- The calculator must provide individualized estimates of net price (cost of attendance minus grant aid) based on what similar students at the institution actually paid

### Loan Information
- A description of the terms and conditions of **Title IV loans** (Direct Subsidized, Direct Unsubsidized, Direct PLUS)
- Information about the **interest rates**, fees, and repayment terms
- A description of **loan forgiveness and cancellation** provisions, including general terms and conditions
- A description of **forbearance** provisions
- The **consequences of defaulting** on a loan, including:
  - Adverse credit reports
  - Federal delinquent debt collection procedures
  - Litigation
  - Wage garnishment
  - Loss of eligibility for other federal aid

### Refund Policy
- The institution's **refund policy** and requirements for the return of Title IV funds
- The requirements and procedures for **officially withdrawing** from the institution

## Required Disclosures — Institutional Information

The statute also requires disclosure of (non-exhaustive list):

- **Accreditation** and approval status
- **Facilities and services** available to students with disabilities
- **Faculty** — number, percentage with terminal degrees
- **Retention and completion rates** (graduation rates by demographic group)
- **Transfer of credit** policies
- **Textbook information** — ISBN and retail price of required course materials (to the extent practicable)
- **Voter registration** information
- **Campus security** (Clery Act disclosures, cross-referenced in Section 1092(f))
- **Athletic program participation rates and financial support** (Equity in Athletics Disclosure Act)

## Loan Counseling Requirements — Section 1092(b) and (l)

### Entrance Counseling
Before the first disbursement of a loan, institutions must provide counseling covering:
- Average anticipated monthly repayment amounts under different repayment plans
- The obligation to repay the full amount
- The borrower's rights and responsibilities
- Available repayment plans, including income-driven repayment
- Debt management strategies
- Information about the National Student Loan Data System (NSLDS)

### Exit Counseling
At departure from the institution (graduation, withdrawal, or less-than-half-time enrollment), institutions must provide counseling covering:
- Repayment plans available and sample monthly payments under each plan
- The difference in interest paid and total payments under each plan
- Debt management strategies
- Loan consolidation options
- Tax benefits related to student loan interest

## Relevance to Desata

### Offer Letters Product
Section 1092(a) establishes the **legal floor** for what institutions must disclose about financial assistance. Desata's work on offer letter standards (in connection with NASFAA's College Cost Transparency Initiative and the GAO-23-104708 findings) should reference these requirements as the minimum baseline.

Key gaps that Desata/ClarifiED can help address:
- Many institutions meet the letter of Section 1092(a) in their catalogs and websites but **fail to present this information clearly in offer letters** (per GAO findings)
- The statute requires the information to be "made readily available" but does not standardize the **format** — this is the gap that NASFAA's CCT initiative and proposed legislation aim to fill
- Loan terms disclosed under Section 1092(a) should be consistent with how loans appear in any recommendation engine output

### ClarifiED Recommendation Engine
- Any loan information presented in ClarifiED must be **consistent with** the disclosure requirements of Section 1092(a)
- ClarifiED's methodology documentation should reference the statutory categories of information that institutions are required to disclose
- If ClarifiED presents cost-of-attendance estimates, they should align with the institution's official COA figures as required by this section

### Compliance Value Proposition
- Institutions already have an obligation under Section 1092(a) to provide clear financial aid information
- ClarifiED can position itself as a tool that helps institutions **meet their existing statutory obligations** more effectively — not as creating new obligations

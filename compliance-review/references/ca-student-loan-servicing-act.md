## Overview

California's Student Loan Servicing Act requires entities engaged in the business of servicing student loans in California to obtain a license from the DFPI. The definition of "servicing" is intentionally broad.

---

## Key Definitions

### Student Loan Servicer (§28104)

> "Student loan servicer" means any person engaged in the business of servicing student loans.

**Exclusions:**
- A debt collector (as defined in Cal. Civ. Code §1788.2) whose business involves collecting **only on defaulted** student loans
- However, debt collectors who **also service nondefaulted loans** as part of their business ARE student loan servicers and need a license

### Servicing (§28100 et seq.)

"Servicing" includes:
1. Receiving any scheduled periodic payments from a borrower, or any notification that a borrower made a scheduled periodic payment, and applying payments to the borrower's account pursuant to the terms of the student loan or the servicing contract
2. During a period when no payment is required on a student loan, maintaining account records for the student loan
3. Interacting with a borrower with the goal of helping the borrower avoid default on the borrower's student loan obligation

> **ClarifiED relevance:** The critical question is whether recommending loans — without receiving payments, maintaining accounts, or interacting to prevent default — constitutes "servicing." Based on the statutory definitions, **loan recommendation alone likely does NOT constitute servicing** under this Act. ClarifiED does not:
> - Receive or apply payments
> - Maintain account records
> - Interact with borrowers to prevent default
>
> However, this analysis should be confirmed with California counsel, particularly given the DFPI's broad interpretive authority.

## Licensing Requirements

### Who Must Be Licensed
Any person engaged in the business of servicing student loans in California, unless exempted.

### Exemptions (§28106)
- Banks and credit unions (and their subsidiaries)
- The United States or any state government
- Entities servicing loans under federal contracts (may have separate registration requirements)
- Other exemptions as specified by the Commissioner

### Application Requirements
- Application through NMLS
- Nonrefundable application fee
- Financial statements demonstrating tangible net worth of **$250,000**
- Surety bond
- Background checks for control persons
- Compliance management system documentation

### Ongoing Requirements
- Annual license renewal through NMLS
- Maintain minimum net worth
- File annual reports
- Submit to examination by DFPI
- Maintain books and records

## Borrower Protections

The Act (as amended by AB 376 in 2020) includes substantive borrower protections:

1. **Prohibition on misrepresentation:** Servicers may not make false or misleading statements regarding borrower accounts
2. **Timely response:** Must respond to borrower inquiries within specified timeframes
3. **Payment processing:** Must apply payments in accordance with borrower instructions
4. **Transfer requirements:** Must provide notice before transferring servicing and ensure no loss of benefits during transfer
5. **Record retention:** Must maintain adequate records
6. **Fair practices:** Must not engage in unfair, deceptive, or abusive acts or practices

---

## Enforcement

- **DFPI** has examination, investigation, and enforcement authority
- May issue orders to cease and desist
- May suspend or revoke licenses
- May impose administrative penalties
- May refer matters for criminal prosecution
- **Private right of action:** Borrowers may bring civil actions for violations

## ClarifiED Analysis

### Likely NOT a "Student Loan Servicer"

Based on the statutory definitions, ClarifiED's loan recommendation engine:
- Does not receive or process borrower payments
- Does not maintain loan account records
- Does not provide default prevention counseling
- Does not hold or own student loans

A loan comparison/recommendation platform that connects borrowers with lender options but does not touch the loan lifecycle is likely outside the scope of "servicing."

### But Watch For:
1. **DFPI interpretive expansions:** The DFPI has broad authority to interpret "servicing" and could expand its reach through rulemaking or enforcement actions
2. **Future amendments:** California has been active in expanding student loan protections; future legislation could capture recommendation platforms
3. **Lender-side licensing:** If ClarifiED is ever compensated by lenders (even indirectly), it could trigger broker-dealer or other licensing requirements separate from this Act
4. **If ClarifiED adds features** like repayment tracking, income-driven repayment comparisons, or servicer interaction tools, those features could cross into "servicing"

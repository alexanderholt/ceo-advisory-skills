# HEOA Preferred Lender List Provisions

**Citation:** 20 U.S.C. §§1019-1019d
**Source:** Higher Education Opportunity Act of 2008, Pub. L. 110-315, Sections 151-155
**Fetched from:** uscode.house.gov

---

## §1019 — Definitions

**Agent:** An officer or employee of a covered institution or institution-affiliated organization.

**Covered Institution:** Any institution of higher education that receives any Federal funding or assistance.

**Education Loan:** Includes loans under federal parts B and D of subchapter IV, plus private education loans.

**Eligible Lender:** References the definition in section 1085(d).

**Institution-Affiliated Organization:** An entity related to a covered institution that recommends or endorses education loans. Examples include alumni groups, athletic organizations, and foundations — but excludes actual lenders.

**Lender:** Varies by loan type: eligible lenders for federal loans, the Secretary for part D loans, and private educational lenders for private loans.

**Officer:** Includes directors or trustees of institutions or affiliated organizations treated as employees.

**Preferred Lender Arrangement:** An arrangement or agreement between a lender and a covered institution under which a lender provides education loans and that relates to such covered institution recommending, promoting, or endorsing the education loan products.

**Private Education Loan:** References section 1650 of title 15.

## §1019a — Responsibilities of Covered Institutions, Institution-Affiliated Organizations, and Lenders

### Institutional Disclosures
Covered institutions and affiliated organizations participating in preferred lender arrangements must disclose federal grant/loan maximums and loan information on websites and materials. For private education loans, institutions must provide Federal Reserve-required disclosures and clarify that federal loans "may be more favorable than the provisions of private education loans."

### Naming Restrictions
Institutions cannot allow lenders to use the school's name, emblem, mascot, or logo in marketing private loans in any way that implies that the loan is offered or made by such institution.

### Lender Responsibilities
Lenders must provide required disclosures before disbursing federal and private education loans. They must annually report to the Secretary any reasonable expenses paid or provided to institutional financial aid staff or affiliated organization agents involved with loan promotion.

### Certification Requirements
By August 2010, lenders participating in preferred arrangements must annually certify compliance with chapter requirements, with auditor attestation as applicable.

## §1019b — Loan Information to Be Disclosed and Model Disclosure Form (Institutions)

### Secretary's Duties
The Secretary must determine minimum information that lenders and institutions must disclose regarding education loans:
- Interest rates and loan terms, including forgiveness and deferment options
- Charges such as origination and default fees, and collection methods
- Annual and aggregate borrowing limits
- Average amounts borrowed by recent graduates by degree type
- Projected interest costs under standard repayment plans
- Consequences of loan default, including bankruptcy implications
- Lender contact information

The Secretary must develop a model disclosure form modeled after Federal Reserve requirements to enable easy comparison between loan products.

### Lender Obligations
Lenders with preferred arrangements must annually provide the Secretary and institutions with required disclosure information for each loan type they plan to offer.

### Institution and Organization Duties
Covered institutions and their affiliated organizations must:
- Provide timely disclosures to prospective borrowers and families in formats allowing informed decisions before loan selection
- Submit annual reports explaining why each preferred lender arrangement benefits students and families
- Comply with code of conduct requirements regarding educational loan practices

## §1019c — Loan Information to Be Disclosed and Model Disclosure Form (Lenders)

### Secretary's Obligation
The Secretary must provide participating institutions with a completed model disclosure form within 180 days of its development. This form includes information about Federal Direct Stafford Loans, Unsubsidized Stafford Loans, and PLUS loans.

### Institution Requirements
- Make the information the Secretary provides available to students attending or planning to attend the institution, or the families of such students
- When offering private education loan information to prospective borrowers, simultaneously provide the federal loan disclosure materials
- May use their own comparable disclosure form instead of the Secretary's model form

---

## §1019d — Self-Certification Form for Private Education Loans

Requires the Secretary of Education to develop a standardized self-certification form for private education loans.

### Form Contents
The form must disclose that applicants may qualify for Federal student financial assistance through a program under subchapter IV or state/institutional aid instead of private loans.

### Required Information
The form includes fields for cost of attendance, estimated financial assistance amounts, and the difference between those two figures.

### Distribution
Institutions must provide this form in written or electronic form, upon request of the applicant.

### Liability Protection
Nothing in this section shall be construed to create a private right of action against institutions regarding this form.

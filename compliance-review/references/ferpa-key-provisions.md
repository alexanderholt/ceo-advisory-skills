# FERPA Key Provisions

**Citation:** Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act, 20 U.S.C. §1232g; 34 C.F.R. Part 99

## Overview

FERPA protects the privacy of student education records at institutions that receive federal funding (virtually all Title IV schools). It gives students (or parents, for minors) rights to access their records and restricts how institutions may disclose personally identifiable information (PII) from education records.

## Key Definitions

### Education Records (20 U.S.C. §1232g(a)(4); 34 C.F.R. §99.3)

Records that are:
1. Directly related to a student; AND
2. Maintained by an educational agency or institution, or by a party acting for the agency or institution.

**Exclusions:** Records of instructional, supervisory, or administrative personnel that are kept in the sole possession of the maker; law enforcement records; employment records (unless employment is based on student status); medical/treatment records; alumni records (post-attendance).

### Personally Identifiable Information (34 C.F.R. §99.3)

Includes but is not limited to: student name, parent name, address, SSN or student ID, date of birth, place of birth, mother's maiden name, biometric records, or other information that alone or in combination is linked or linkable to a specific student.

### Directory Information (34 C.F.R. §99.3)

Information that would not generally be considered harmful if disclosed. May include: name, address, telephone, email, date and place of birth, major field of study, enrollment status, dates of attendance, degrees received, participation in activities, most recently attended institution.

Institutions must give annual notice of what they designate as directory information and allow students to opt out.

## Consent Requirement (34 C.F.R. §99.30)

General rule: Prior written consent of the student (or parent) is required before disclosing PII from education records. Consent must:
- Specify records to be disclosed
- State the purpose of the disclosure
- Identify the party or class of parties to whom disclosure is made
- Be signed and dated

## Exceptions to Consent — Relevant to ClarifiED

### School Officials Exception (34 C.F.R. §99.31(a)(1))

An institution may disclose education records without consent to **school officials with legitimate educational interests**, including contractors, consultants, volunteers, or other parties to whom the institution has outsourced institutional services or functions.

**Requirements for a third-party contractor to qualify:**

1. The outside party **performs an institutional service or function** for which the institution would otherwise use employees
2. The outside party is **under the direct control** of the institution with respect to the use and maintenance of education records
3. The outside party is **subject to the requirements** governing the use and redisclosure of PII from education records (i.e., may not redisclose without consent)

**The institution must:**
- Include contractors in its annual FERPA notification as parties who may be considered school officials
- Specify criteria for determining "legitimate educational interest" in its annual notification

> **ClarifiED relevance:** If a school contracts with ClarifiED to provide loan recommendation services, ClarifiED could potentially access student financial aid data under this exception — provided the contract satisfies all three prongs above. The institution retains responsibility for ensuring compliance.

### Other Potentially Relevant Exceptions

- **§99.31(a)(3):** Disclosure in connection with financial aid — An institution may disclose PII to authorized representatives in connection with financial aid for which the student has applied or received, if the information is necessary to determine eligibility, amount, conditions, or enforce terms.
- **§99.31(a)(4):** Disclosure to organizations conducting studies for or on behalf of the institution — Must have written agreement specifying purpose, scope, data elements, and destruction timeline.
- **§99.31(a)(6):** Disclosure to accrediting organizations.

### De-identified Data (34 C.F.R. §99.31(b))

An institution may release education records without consent if all PII has been removed and the institution has made a reasonable determination that a student's identity is not personally identifiable, whether through single or combined disclosures, and taking into account other reasonably available information.

## Redisclosure Restrictions (34 C.F.R. §99.33)

A party that receives PII from education records may use it **only for the purpose for which the disclosure was made**. The recipient must not redisclose PII to other parties without consent, except in narrow circumstances.

If ClarifiED receives student data under the school officials exception, ClarifiED:
- Cannot share that data with lenders in identifiable form
- Cannot use data for purposes beyond the contracted service
- Must return or destroy data when the contract ends (per institutional policy)

## Enforcement

- Complaints filed with the Family Policy Compliance Office (FPCO) at ED
- Sanction: Potential loss of federal funding (nuclear option, rarely invoked)
- No private right of action under FERPA (confirmed by Supreme Court in *Gonzaga University v. Doe*, 536 U.S. 273 (2002))

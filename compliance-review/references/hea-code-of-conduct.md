# HEA Code of Conduct for Education Loans

**Citation:** 20 U.S.C. §1094(a)(25) and §1094(e)
**Source:** Higher Education Act, as amended by HEOA (2008)
**Fetched from:** uscode.house.gov

---

## §1094(a)(25) — Code of Conduct Requirement

Institutions participating in loan programs must establish a code of conduct addressing education loans that:

- Prohibits conflicts of interest among officers, employees, and agents
- Includes minimum provisions detailed in subsection (e)
- Must be published prominently on the institution's website
- Requires annual training for all personnel with loan responsibilities

---

## §1094(e) — Code of Conduct Requirements (Seven Prohibitions)

### 1. Revenue-Sharing Ban
Institutions cannot arrange with lenders where the lender compensates the school based on loan volume or recommendations.

### 2. Gift Prohibitions
Financial aid staff and agents cannot solicit or accept gifts from lenders, with narrow exceptions for training materials and professional development.

### 3. Contracting Restrictions
Loan-related personnel cannot receive consulting fees or financial benefits from lenders, with limited board service exceptions involving conflict-of-interest policies.

### 4. Borrower Interaction
Institutions cannot assign first-time borrowers to specific lenders or delay certification based on lender selection.

### 5. Private Loan Restrictions
Schools cannot request lender funds for private education loans in exchange for loan volume commitments.

### 6. Staffing Assistance
Institutions cannot request call center or financial aid office staffing from lenders, except short-term emergency assistance.

### 7. Advisory Board Compensation
Employees serving on lender advisory boards cannot receive compensation beyond reimbursement for reasonable expenses.

## Relevance to Desata

ClarifiED must be structured so that institutions using it do not violate their code of conduct. Key implications:
- No revenue sharing between Desata and lenders based on loan volume or recommendations (Prohibition 1)
- No gifts or financial benefits flowing from lenders through Desata to institutional staff (Prohibition 2)
- No assignment of borrowers to specific lenders (Prohibition 4)
- No loan volume commitments in exchange for participation (Prohibition 5)

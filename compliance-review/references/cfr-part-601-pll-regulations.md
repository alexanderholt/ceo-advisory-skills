## Subpart A — General

### §601.1 Scope

This part establishes disclosure and reporting requirements for covered institutions, institution-affiliated organizations, and lenders that provide, issue, recommend, promote, endorse, or provide information relating to education loans. Education loans include loans authorized by the Higher Education Act of 1965, as amended (HEA) and private education loans.

### §601.2 Definitions

**Agent:** An officer or employee of a covered institution or an institution-affiliated organization.

**Covered institution:** Any institution of higher education, proprietary institution of higher education, postsecondary vocational institution, or institution outside the United States, as these terms are defined in 34 CFR part 600, that receives any Federal funding or assistance.

**Education loan:** Except when used as part of the term "private education loan":
1. Any loan made, insured, or guaranteed under the Federal Family Education Loan (FFEL) Program;
2. Any loan made under the William D. Ford Federal Direct Loan Program; or
3. A private education loan.

**Institution-affiliated organization:**
1. Any organization that:
   - Is directly or indirectly related to a covered institution; and
   - Is engaged in the practice of recommending, promoting, or endorsing education loans for students attending such covered institution or the families of such students.
2. May include an alumni organization, athletic organization, foundation, or social, academic, or professional organization, of a covered institution; and
3. Does not include any lender with respect to any education loan secured, made, or extended by such lender.

**Lender:**
1. An eligible lender in the FFEL Program, as defined in 34 CFR 682.200(b);
2. The Department in the Direct Loan program;
3. In the case of a private educational loan, a private education lender as defined in section 140 of the Truth in Lending Act; and
4. Any other person engaged in the business of securing, making, or extending education loans on behalf of the lender.

**Officer:** A director or trustee of a covered institution or institution-affiliated organization, if such individual is treated as an employee of such covered institution or institution-affiliated organization, respectively.

**Preferred lender arrangement:**
1. An arrangement or agreement between a lender and a covered institution or an institution-affiliated organization of such covered institution:
   - Under which a lender provides or otherwise issues education loans to the students attending such covered institution or the families of such students; and
   - That relates to such covered institution or such institution-affiliated organization recommending, promoting, or endorsing the education loan products of the lender.
2. Does not include:
   - Arrangements or agreements with respect to loans made under the William D. Ford Federal Direct Loan Program; or
   - Arrangements or agreements with respect to loans that originate through the PLUS Loan auction pilot program under section 499(b) of the HEA.
3. An arrangement or agreement does not exist if the private education loan provided or issued to a student attending a covered institution is made by the covered institution or by an institution-affiliated organization of the covered institution, and the private education loan is:
   - Funded by the covered institution's or institution-affiliated organization's own funds;
   - Funded by donor-directed contributions;
   - Made under title VII or title VIII of the Public Service Health Act; or
   - Made under a State-funded financial aid program, if the terms and conditions of the loan include a loan forgiveness option for public service.

**Private education loan:** As the term is defined in 12 CFR 226.46(b)(5), a loan provided by a private educational lender that is not a title IV loan and that is issued expressly for postsecondary education expenses to a borrower, regardless of whether the loan is provided through the educational institution that the student attends or directly to the borrower from the private educational lender. Does not include:
1. An extension of credit under an open end consumer credit plan, a reverse mortgage transaction, a residential mortgage transaction, or any other loan that is secured by real property or a dwelling; or
2. An extension of credit in which the educational institution is the lender if:
   - The term of the extension of credit is 90 days or less; or
   - An interest rate will not be applied to the credit balance and the term of the extension of credit is one year or less, even if the credit is payable in more than four installments.

## Subpart B — Loan Information To Be Disclosed by Covered Institutions and Institution-Affiliated Organizations

### §601.10 Preferred lender arrangement disclosures

**(a)** A covered institution, or an institution-affiliated organization, that participates in a preferred lender arrangement must disclose:

**On website and in all informational materials that describe or discuss education loans:**
1. The maximum amount of Federal grant and loan aid under title IV of the HEA available to students, in an easy to understand format;
2. The information identified on a model disclosure form developed by the Secretary pursuant to section 153(a)(2)(B) of the HEA, for each type of education loan offered pursuant to a preferred lender arrangement; and
3. A statement that such institution is required to process the documents required to obtain a loan under the FFEL Program from any eligible lender the student selects.

**On website and in all informational materials that describe or discuss private education loans:**
1. For covered institutions: the information that the Board of Governors of the Federal Reserve System requires to be disclosed under section 128(e)(11) of the Truth in Lending Act (15 U.S.C. 1638(e)(11)), for each type of private education loan offered pursuant to a preferred lender arrangement; and
2. For institution-affiliated organizations: the information the Board of Governors requires to be disclosed under section 128(e)(1) of the Truth in Lending Act (15 U.S.C. 1638(e)(1)).

**(b)** Informational materials are publications, mailings, or electronic messages or materials that:
1. Are distributed to prospective or current students and families; and
2. Describe or discuss the financial aid opportunities available to students.

**(c)** Information must be provided to students or families annually and in a manner that allows them to take such information into account before selecting a lender or applying for an education loan.

**(d)** If a covered institution compiles, maintains, and makes available a preferred lender list as required under §668.14(b)(28), the institution must:

1. **Clearly and fully disclose on the preferred lender list:**
   - Not less than the information required to be disclosed under section 153(a)(2)(A) of the HEA;
   - Why the institution participates in a preferred lender arrangement with each lender on the list, particularly with respect to terms and conditions or provisions favorable to the borrower; and
   - That students do not have to borrow from a lender on the preferred lender list.

2. **Ensure minimum lender diversity:**
   - Not less than three FFEL lenders that are not affiliates of each other on the list; and
   - If the institution recommends private education loans, not less than two lenders of private education loans that are not affiliates of each other on the list;
   - Must indicate for each listed lender whether the lender is or is not an affiliate of each other lender, and if so, describe the details of such affiliation.

3. **Prominently disclose the selection method and criteria** used to ensure lenders are selected on the basis of the best interests of the borrowers, including:
   - Payment of origination or other fees on behalf of the borrower;
   - Highly competitive interest rates, or other terms and conditions;
   - High-quality servicing; or
   - Additional benefits beyond standard terms.

4. **Exercise a duty of care and a duty of loyalty** to compile the preferred lender list without prejudice and for the sole benefit of the students attending the institution, or the families of such students.

5. **Not deny or otherwise impede the borrower's choice of a lender** or cause unnecessary delay in loan certification for those borrowers who choose a lender not on the preferred lender list.

### §601.11 Private education loan disclosures and self-certification form

**(a)** A covered institution or institution-affiliated organization that provides information regarding a private education loan from a lender to a prospective borrower must provide private education loan disclosures, regardless of whether it participates in a preferred lender arrangement.

**(b)** The disclosures must:
1. Provide the prospective borrower with the information the Federal Reserve Board requires to be disclosed under section 128(e)(1) of the Truth in Lending Act;
2. Inform the prospective borrower that:
   - They may qualify for loans or other assistance under title IV of the HEA; and
   - The terms and conditions of Title IV loans may be more favorable than private education loans.

**(c)** Information regarding private education loans must be presented in a manner distinct from Title IV loan information.

**(d)** Upon request, an institution must provide:
1. The self-certification form for private education loans developed by the Secretary; and
2. The information required to complete the form, to the extent the institution possesses such information.

### §601.12 Use of institution and lender name

A covered institution or institution-affiliated organization that participates in a preferred lender arrangement regarding private education loans must:

**(a)** Not agree to the lender's use of the name, emblem, mascot, or logo of such institution or organization in the marketing of private education loans in any way that implies that the loan is offered or made by such institution or organization instead of the lender; and

**(b)** Ensure that the name of the lender is displayed in all information and documentation related to the private education loans.

## Subpart C — Responsibilities of Covered Institutions and Institution-Affiliated Organizations

### §601.20 Annual report

Each covered institution and institution-affiliated organization that participates in a preferred lender arrangement must:

**(a)** Prepare and submit to the Secretary an annual report that includes, for each lender:
1. The disclosure information described in §601.10(c); and
2. A detailed explanation of why such institution or organization participates in a preferred lender arrangement with the lender, including why the terms, conditions, and provisions of each type of education loan are beneficial for students or families.

**(b)** Ensure the report is made available to the public and provided to students attending or planning to attend the institution and their families.

### §601.21 Code of conduct

**(a)** A covered institution that participates in a preferred lender arrangement must:
1. Develop a code of conduct with respect to FFEL Program loans and private education loans;
2. The code must prohibit conflicts of interest and include the provisions specified in paragraph (c);
3. Publish the code prominently on the institution's website; and
4. Annually inform all agents with loan responsibilities of the provisions.

**(b)** Institution-affiliated organizations must comply with and publish the code of conduct.

**(c)** The code of conduct must prohibit:

**1. Revenue-sharing arrangements with any lender.** The institution must not enter into any revenue-sharing arrangement — defined as an arrangement where a lender provides loans to students and the institution recommends the lender, and in exchange the lender pays a fee or provides other material benefits, including revenue or profit sharing, to the institution or an agent.

**2. Employees of the financial aid office receiving gifts from a lender, guarantor, or loan servicer.** Agents with loan responsibilities must not solicit or accept any gift from a lender, guarantor, or servicer. "Gift" means any gratuity, favor, discount, entertainment, hospitality, loan, or other item having a monetary value of more than a de minimis amount.

Exceptions include:
- Standard material on issues related to a loan, default aversion, or financial literacy (brochures, workshops, training);
- Food, refreshments, training, or informational material furnished as an integral part of a training session designed to improve service, if it contributes to professional development;
- Favorable loan terms provided to a student employed by the institution if comparable to terms provided to all students;
- Entrance and exit counseling services, as long as institution staff are in control and the counseling does not promote specific lender products;
- Philanthropic contributions unrelated to education loans or not made in exchange for any advantage related to loans;
- State education grants, scholarships, or financial aid funds.

A gift to a family member of an agent is considered a gift to the agent if given with the agent's knowledge and because of the agent's official position.

**3. Consulting or other contracting arrangements.** Financial aid staff must not accept from any lender any fee, payment, or other financial benefit (including stock purchase opportunity) as compensation for consulting or other contracts related to education loans. Exceptions exist for board service with conflict-of-interest policies.

**4. Directing borrowers to particular lenders or delaying loan certifications.** The institution must not:
- Assign first-time borrowers to a particular lender through award packaging or other methods; or
- Refuse to certify, or delay certification of, any loan based on the borrower's selection of a particular lender or guaranty agency.

**5. Offers of funds for private loans.** The institution must not request or accept from any lender any offer of funds to be used for private education loans, including opportunity pool loans, in exchange for providing the lender with a specified number or volume of loans, or a preferred lender arrangement. An "opportunity pool loan" means a private education loan involving a payment by the institution of points, premiums, additional interest, or financial support to the lender for the purpose of extending credit.

**6. Staffing assistance.** The institution must not request or accept from any lender any assistance with call center staffing or financial aid office staffing, except:
- Professional development training for financial aid administrators;
- Educational counseling materials, financial literacy materials, or debt management materials (with lender identification disclosed); or
- Staffing services on a short-term, nonrecurring basis during emergencies (State-declared or Federally declared natural disasters, etc.).

**7. Advisory board compensation.** Financial aid employees serving on advisory boards established by lenders or guarantors must not receive anything of value except reimbursement for reasonable expenses.

## Subpart D — Loan Information To Be Disclosed by Institutions Participating in the William D. Ford Direct Loan Program

### §601.30 Duties of institutions

**(a)** Each covered institution participating in the Direct Loan Program must:
1. Make the information identified in the Secretary's model disclosure form available to students and families; and
2. If the institution provides information regarding a private education loan, concurrently provide the borrower with the Secretary's model disclosure form information.

**(b)** A covered institution may use a comparable form designed by the institution instead of the model disclosure form.

## Subpart E — Lender Responsibilities

### §601.40 Disclosure and reporting requirements for lenders

**(a) Disclosures to borrowers:**
1. FFEL lenders must provide borrowers with required disclosures under §682.205(a) and (b) at or prior to disbursement, in writing (including electronic), in clear and understandable terms.
2. Private education lenders must comply with section 128(e) of the Truth in Lending Act (15 U.S.C. 1638(e)).

**(b) Reports to the Secretary:** Each FFEL lender must report annually:
1. Any reasonable expenses paid or provided to any agent of a covered institution who is employed in the financial aid office or has loan responsibilities, for service on a lender advisory board;
2. Any similar expenses paid to agents of institution-affiliated organizations;
3. Reports must include: the amount, the name of the agent, the dates of activity, and a brief description.

**(c) Lender certification of compliance:**
1. Any FFEL lender participating in preferred lender arrangements must annually certify compliance with the HEA;
2. If an audit is required under 34 CFR 682.305(c), compliance must be attested to by the lender's auditor;
3. Lenders not required to submit an audit must submit certification as directed by the Secretary.

**(d) Annual lender report to covered institutions:** FFEL lenders with preferred lender arrangements must annually provide to the covered institution or institution-affiliated organization and to the Secretary the information required in relation to the FFEL loans planned for the next award year.
